Paco de Lucía

The master

Paco de Lucía playing effortlessly

A timeline of the life of Paco de Lucía:

  • 1947 - Born Francísco Sánchez Gómez in December in Alegiras, Spain
  • 1961 - At the age of 14 he made his first record with his brother Pepe, Los Chiquitos de Algeciras (Kids of Algeciras).
  • 1963 - In the early 1960s, de Lucía toured with the flamenco troupe of dancer José Greco
  • 1964 - With Ricardo Modrego he recorded three albums: Dos guitarras flamencas (1964), 12 canciones de García Lorca para guitarra and 12 éxitos para 2 guitarras flamencas (1965)
  • 1966 - He toured again with José Greco
  • 1967 - Recorded his debut solo album, La fabulosa guitarra de Paco de Lucía and appeared at the 1967 Berlin Jazz Festival. He recorded albums with his brother, including Canciones andaluzas para 2 guitarras (1967) and Dos guitarras flamencas en América Latina
  • 1969 - Recorded Fantasía flamenca de Paco de Lucía and 12 Hits para 2 guitarras flamencas y orquesta de cuerda
  • 1975 - Became the first-ever flamenco performer to perform at the Teatro Real of Madrid
  • 1976 - Recorded Almoraima
  • 1977 - Performed with Carlos Santana in the Plaza de toros de las Arenas bullring in Barcelona. He was invited by Al Di Meola to record on his "Mediterranean Sundance" piece for his album Elegant Gypsy
  • 1978 - Paco and his brothers recorded Interpreta a Manuel de Falla
  • 1979 - Paco de Lucía, John McLaughlin, and Larry Coryell formed The Guitar Trio and together made a tour of Europe
  • 1981 - Coryell was replaced with Di Meola, and The Guitar Trio released one of their most successful records, Friday Night in San Francisco, which sold over 1 million copies and generated a significant interest in flamenco music in America and Europe. De Lucía also formed the Paco de Lucía Sextet (which included his brothers Ramón and Pepe), and released the first of its three albums that same year
  • 1982 - Paco put on a series of concerts with jazz pianist Chick Corea
  • 1984 - Recorded and produced the album, Live... One Summer Night and accepted a position as a judge at Seville's 1984 Bienal
  • 1986 - Paco de Lucía would continue to perform with McLaughlin as a duo across Europe in 1986 and later
  • 1987 - Paco performed for the first time in the Soviet Union, and went back to his roots with his highly successful release, Siroco. Siroco is often cited as his best album and one of the greatest flamenco albums of all time
  • 1990 - The sextet got together to record Zyryab, a groundbreaking Arabic flamenco/jazz album with jazz pianist Chick Corea and fellow virtuoso flamenco guitarist Manolo Sanlúcar
  • 1991 - Asked to perform and interpret Joaquín Rodrigo's Concierto de Aranjuez
  • 1992 - Performed live at the bullring at Seville Expo '92
  • 1995 - He and Bryan Adams recorded the hit song and video Have You Ever Really Loved A Woman on the soundtrack for the American film Don Juan DeMarco
  • 2004 - He toured the United States and Canada with Seville flamenco singer La Tana and released Cositas Buenas and won the Prince of Asturias Awards in Arts
  • 2005 - He was nominated for producer of the year by the Latin Grammy for La Tana's "Tu, Ven a Mi"
  • 2010 - He was awarded an honorary doctorate by Berklee College of Music in Boston, and performed at the Montreux Festival
  • 2014 - Paco de Lucía died of a heart attack on 25 February 2014

“Paco was a real man. Real in the sense of true; to himself, to his music and to everyone else. A man in that he was passionate, but had true compassion and deep understanding of the human condition.."

John McLaughlin
