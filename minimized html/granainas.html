Granaínas

The granaínas are an adaption of the fandangos grandes which have been strongly influenced by the Moors, rulers of Granada for eight centuries. They have, therefore, developed a more discordant, Oriental quality than the fandangos grandes. They are a free cante without a determined compás. The term “granainás” is an abbreviation of “granadinas”, which means to say “songs from Granada”.
